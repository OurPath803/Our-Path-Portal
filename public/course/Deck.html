2.1 · Framework overview

Development sequenced into six phases

Each phase carries a distinct focus, a set of activated fields, and a named transition that marks progression.

  • The work moves from orientation through excavation and clarification toward building and embedding.
  • Phases are not rigid stages. A person may revisit earlier ground as new material surfaces.
  • The final phase, Transmission, turns the person from receiving support to offering it.
