Preface 
OurPath Guidance operates in the space between traditional mosque provision and clinical therapy. The developmental model documented in this handbook constitutes the intellectual and practical architecture through which that space is navigated — with precision, with care, and with grounding in both Islamic psychospiritual tradition and contemporary developmental science. 
This handbook is not a manual. It is a reference architecture. Each section maps a distinct layer of the model: the philosophical commitments that underpin it, the sequential framework through which it operates, the fields and domains it activates, and the convergences between Islamic and Western frameworks it draws upon. 
وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا ۝ فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا 
Wa nafsin wa ma sawwaha, fa-alhamaha fujuraha wa taqwaha 
By the soul and that which fashioned it — and inspired it with its depravity and its piety. [Ash-Shams: 7-8] 

Section 1: Philosophical Foundations 
1.1 The Core Proposition 
The human being is not primarily a problem to be solved. The human being is a trust (amanah) to be unfolded — gradually, intentionally, and in relationship. OurPath&apos;s developmental model is built on this premise. 
Growth, in this framework, is not measured by output alone. It is measured by the alignment between who a person is becoming and the purpose they were created to embody. The model therefore works at the intersection of identity, agency, and direction. 
1.2 The Tazkiyah Principle 
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن زَكَّاهَا ۝ وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّاهَا 
Qad aflaha man zakkaha, wa qad khaba man dassaha 
Successful is the one who purifies it. And failed is the one who suppresses it. [Ash-Shams: 9-10] 
Tazkiyah — the purification and cultivation of the nafs (self) — is the classical Islamic framework for human development. It is not a therapeutic modality. It is an ontological orientation: the soul has a trajectory, and the work of development is to align daily life with that trajectory. 
OurPath draws from this tradition not as religious instruction but as a structural lens. The stages of the nafs (nafs al-ammara, nafs al-lawwama, nafs al-mutma&apos;inna) function as developmental markers: where is this person&apos;s inner life pulling them, and toward what?

Section 7: The Cost Audit &amp; Integration Filter 
7.1 The Cost Audit 
Every developmental commitment carries a cost. The Cost Audit ensures that the person understands, accepts, and plans for what change will actually require of them. It prevents the collapse of well-intentioned plans through unexamined cost. 
Cost Domain 
Questions 
Islamic Frame 
Time 
What time will this require? What currently occupies that time? What must be released? 
Waqt — time as sacred trust. Every hour is an amanah. 
Energy 
What will this ask of the person physically, emotionally, and cognitively? What currently depletes them? 
Himma — aspirational energy is finite and must be invested wisely. 
Relationships 
Who will be affected by this change? Who will resist it? Who must be negotiated with? 
Huquq — the rights of others constrain individual development. 
Identity 
What version of self must be released for the new to emerge? What is the grief in that? 
Nafs — the transition between stations of the nafs is not painless. 
Financial 
What economic implications does this change carry? What is the person&apos;s current financial capacity? 
Rizq — provision is from Allah, but planning is from the person. 
Spiritual 
Does this commitment strengthen or weaken the person&apos;s connection to their values and worship? 
Taqwa — any path that distances from God requires examination. 

7.2 The Integration Filter 
The Integration Filter is applied before any commitment is finalised. It asks whether the proposed change is coherent with the person&apos;s existing life architecture or whether it requires restructuring before it can be embedded. 
Filter Question 
Pass Condition 
Fail Condition — Action Required 
Does this fit within my values? 
The commitment aligns with stated values and does not require compromise. 
Return to values clarification. Resolve the conflict before proceeding. 
Does this align with my purpose? 
The commitment moves toward the person&apos;s stated directional horizon. 
Examine whether the horizon is correct or whether the commitment needs reframing. 
Can I sustain this? 
The person has sufficient capacity (time, energy, support) to maintain this over the relevant horizon. 
Conduct Cost Audit. Reduce scope or build capacity first. 
Have I accounted for the costs? 
All major costs have been named and accepted. 
Return to Cost Audit. Name what has been avoided. 
Does this strengthen or damage key relationships? 
The commitment is either neutral to or strengthening of the person&apos;s most important relationships. 
Map relationship implications. Develop a communication plan. 
Am I doing this from agency or reaction? 
The commitment arises from considered intention, not fear, social pressure, or impulsive resolution. 
Apply motivational inquiry. Surface the real driver before proceeding.

Section 10: Mirror-Reading Reflective Architecture 
Mirror-Reading is the practitioner&apos;s primary interpretive method. It is grounded in the classical Islamic concept of the qalb (heart) as a mirror — capable of reflecting reality with clarity when polished, and distorting it when covered. 
10.1 The Three Layers of Reading 
Layer 
What Is Being Read 
Method 
Islamic Grounding 
Surface 
What the person presents: their words, the content of their account, their stated concerns. 
Listening, clarifying, summarising.

Pattern 
What recurs: the recurring themes, the words used repeatedly, the narrative structures the person reaches for. 
Pattern-tracking, reflective questioning, gentle naming. 
Muhasaba — discerning the habitual tendencies of the self. 
Depth

Section 12: The Three-Tier Delivery Model 
The OurPath developmental model is delivered through three distinct channels. Each tier serves a different function and reaches the person at a different depth and frequency. 
Tier 
Format 
Frequency 
Function 
Primary Mechanism 
Tier 1 
Reflective Journal 
Daily / Weekly 
Self-directed development. The person works the model independently through structured reflection prompts. 
Muhasaba — self-audit. The journal as a practitioner when no practitioner is present. 
Tier 2 
Workshops &amp; Group Sessions 
Monthly / Quarterly 
Community-based development. The model is applied in facilitated group settings, drawing on suhba and collective wisdom. 
Suhba — the developmental power of being in the company of those who are also seeking. 
Tier 3 
1-to-1 Mentoring 
Weekly / Fortnightly 
Depth developmental work. The full model is applied in the dyadic relationship, session by session, over a structured package. 
The relationship as the primary vehicle. All other instruments serve this encounter. 

12.1 Tier Interaction 
The three tiers are designed to be mutually reinforcing. A person in Tier 3 mentoring is encouraged to use the Tier 1 journal between sessions and may be introduced to Tier 2 workshops at appropriate phases of their development.
